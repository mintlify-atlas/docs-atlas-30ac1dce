# Quick Start Guide

This guide will help you get sunnypilot up and running in your vehicle as quickly as possible.

<Note>
  If you prefer more detailed instructions, check out the [Installation Guide](/installation).
</Note>

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Compatible Vehicle">
    Verify your vehicle is on the [list of 336+ supported cars](https://community.sunnypilot.ai/t/getting-started-using-sunnypilot-in-your-supported-car/251). Your car must have:
    - Factory adaptive cruise control (ACC) or equivalent
    - Lane keeping assist (LKAS) or lane centering
    - Model year typically 2016 or newer (varies by make/model)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="comma Device">
    Purchase a comma device from [comma.ai](https://comma.ai/shop):
    - **comma four** (recommended) - Latest hardware with improved power efficiency
    - **comma 3/3X** - Previous generation, fully supported
    
    <Warning>
      Make sure to order the correct wiring harness for your specific vehicle when purchasing from comma.ai.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Installation Hardware">
    Ensure you have the appropriate hardware for your vehicle:
    - Vehicle-specific wiring harness (comes with comma device purchase)
    - Mounting solution (included with comma device)
    - OBD-C cable (included)
    - comma power v3 (recommended for most vehicles)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Installation Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Physical Installation">
    Install your comma device in your vehicle:
    
    1. Mount the comma device behind your rearview mirror with a clear view of the road
    2. Connect the vehicle-specific harness between your car's camera connector and the comma device
    3. Plug the OBD-C cable into your vehicle's OBD-II port
    4. Route cables neatly to avoid interference with driving controls
    
    <Tip>
      Refer to vehicle-specific installation guides on the [comma.ai shop](https://comma.ai/shop) for your exact model.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Power On & Setup">
    When you first power on your comma device:
    
    1. Wait for the device to boot (first boot may take a few minutes)
    2. Connect to WiFi when prompted
    3. Create or log in to your comma account
    4. Accept the terms of service
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Install sunnypilot">
    Flash sunnypilot to your comma device:
    
    1. From the device settings, select **Software**
    2. Choose **Uninstall openpilot**
    3. After uninstall completes, you'll see the setup screen
    4. Enter a custom software URL when prompted:
    
    ```bash
    https://smiskol.com/fork/sunnyhaibin
    ```
    
    5. Select your preferred branch (see [Branch Selection](/installation#branch-selection) for details):
       - **honda_c_stable** - Stable release (recommended for most users)
       - **RC** - Release candidate with latest tested features  
       - **dev** - Development branch (for testing, may be unstable)
    
    <Note>
      The installation process will download and flash sunnypilot. This may take several minutes depending on your internet connection.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Calibration">
    After installation, sunnypilot needs to calibrate:
    
    1. Drive on a straight highway or road
    2. Keep the lane lines visible to the camera
    3. Calibration typically completes within 1-2 miles of driving
    4. The device will notify you when calibration is complete
    
    <Warning>
      Do not attempt to engage sunnypilot until calibration is complete.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="First Drive">
    Once calibration is complete, you're ready to use sunnypilot:
    
    1. Start driving and enable your car's adaptive cruise control
    2. sunnypilot will automatically engage when conditions are met:
       - Vehicle speed above minimum threshold (varies by vehicle)
       - Lane lines clearly visible
       - Driver monitoring system active
    3. Keep your hands on the wheel and eyes on the road at all times
    4. You can disengage at any time by:
       - Pressing the brake pedal
       - Turning the steering wheel
       - Pressing the cancel button on your cruise control stalk
    
    <Tip>
      Start with shorter drives on familiar roads to get comfortable with sunnypilot's behavior.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Expect

### Driver Monitoring

sunnypilot uses the driver-facing camera to ensure you remain attentive:

- Keep your eyes on the road
- The system will alert you if it detects distraction or drowsiness
- Repeated warnings may temporarily disable sunnypilot

### Steering Control

- sunnypilot provides smooth lane centering
- Always keep your hands on the wheel
- Be ready to take control at any time
- The system works best on highways with clear lane markings

### Speed Control

- Adaptive cruise control maintains speed and following distance
- Some vehicles use stock ACC, others use sunnypilot's longitudinal control
- You can adjust following distance using your car's controls

## Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="sunnypilot won't engage">
    Common causes:
    - Calibration not complete - continue driving on straight roads
    - Speed below minimum threshold - accelerate to highway speeds
    - Lane lines not visible - ensure clear road markings
    - Driver monitoring alerts - keep eyes on road
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Device keeps rebooting">
    Possible solutions:
    - Check all cable connections are secure
    - Ensure OBD-II port connection is firm
    - Verify you have the correct harness for your vehicle
    - Check for software update availability
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Poor lane keeping">
    Try these steps:
    - Verify camera lens is clean and unobstructed
    - Re-check device mounting position and angle
    - Complete calibration process fully
    - Ensure windshield is clean
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

For more help, visit the [sunnypilot community forum](https://community.sunnypilot.ai/).
