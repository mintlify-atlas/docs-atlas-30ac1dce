To run sunnypilot in your vehicle, you need compatible hardware that interfaces with your car's systems. The primary device is the **comma four** (formerly sold as comma 3X), along with vehicle-specific harnesses and mounting hardware.

## Core Hardware Components

### comma four Device

The comma four is the main computing device that runs sunnypilot. It's a purpose-built Linux computer with integrated sensors and connectivity.

<img src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/47793918/233812617-beab2e71-57b9-479e-8bff-c3931347ca40.png" alt="comma four device" />

#### Technical Specifications

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Processor & Performance">
    - **SoC**: Qualcomm Snapdragon (high-performance mobile processor)
    - **RAM**: 8GB LPDDR4X
    - **Storage**: 128GB UFS 3.1
    - **Operating System**: AGNOS (custom Linux-based OS)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Cameras & Sensors">
    - **Road-Facing Camera**: Wide-angle camera for lane detection and vehicle tracking
    - **Driver-Facing Camera**: Monitors driver attention (Driver Monitoring)
    - **Wide-Angle Camera**: Enhanced field of view for better situational awareness
    - **GPS**: Integrated high-precision GPS
    - **IMU**: Inertial Measurement Unit for vehicle dynamics
    - **Magnetometer**: For heading and orientation
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Connectivity">
    - **LTE**: Built-in cellular connectivity for updates and comma connect
    - **Wi-Fi**: 802.11ac dual-band
    - **Bluetooth**: 5.0
    - **USB-C**: Main OBD-C connection port
    - **Aux USB-C**: For accessories and debugging
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Display & Interface">
    - **Screen**: 6.67" 1080p touchscreen display
    - **User Interface**: Touch-based controls
    - **Thermal Management**: Active cooling system
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Power">
    - **Input Voltage**: 12V DC (vehicle power)
    - **Power Consumption**: ~10-15W during operation
    - **Thermal Operating Range**: -20°C to 60°C ambient
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
The comma four is designed specifically for automotive use with robust thermal management and automotive-grade power handling.
</Info>

### Vehicle Harness

A vehicle-specific harness connects the comma four to your car's systems. Each supported vehicle requires a specific harness type.

#### Harness Types by Manufacturer

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Honda/Acura">
    **Honda Nidec Connector**
    - For 2016-2019 Honda/Acura vehicles
    - Connects to Nidec radar system
    - Example: 2016-18 Honda Civic
    
    **Honda Bosch A Connector**
    - For 2017-2022 Honda/Acura vehicles
    - Connects to Bosch radar system
    - Example: 2018-22 Honda Accord
    
    **Honda Bosch B Connector**
    - For 2022-2024 Honda vehicles
    - Newer Bosch system
    - Example: 2022-24 Honda Civic
    
    **Honda Bosch C Connector**
    - For 2023+ Honda/Acura vehicles
    - Latest generation system
    - Example: 2023-25 Honda Accord
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Hyundai/Kia/Genesis">
    **Hyundai A-Q Connectors**
    - Multiple connector types (A through Q)
    - Specific to vehicle model and year
    - Each letter corresponds to different CAN bus configuration
    
    Examples:
    - Hyundai A: 2020-23 Sonata
    - Hyundai K: 2022 Azera
    - Hyundai P: Ioniq 6 with HDA II
    - Hyundai Q: Ioniq 5 with HDA II
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Toyota/Lexus">
    **Toyota A Connector**
    - Used for most Toyota/Lexus vehicles
    - Compatible with Toyota Safety Sense systems
    - Example: 2019-25 Lexus ES
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Ford">
    **Ford Q3 Connector**
    - For 2018-2024 Ford vehicles
    - Example: 2020-22 Ford Escape
    
    **Ford Q4 Connector**
    - For 2021+ Ford vehicles with newer platform
    - Requires long OBD-C cable (9.5 ft)
    - Example: 2021-23 Ford F-150
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="GM">
    **GM Connector**
    - Standard for Chevrolet, GMC, Cadillac
    - Example: 2022-23 Chevrolet Bolt EUV
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="FCA (Chrysler/Dodge/Jeep)">
    **FCA Connector**
    - For Chrysler, Dodge, Jeep vehicles
    - Example: 2017-25 Chrysler Pacifica
    
    **Ram Connector**
    - Specific to Ram trucks
    - Example: 2019-24 Ram 1500
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Volkswagen/Audi">
    **VW J533 Connector**
    - Gateway harness for MQB platform vehicles
    - Requires long OBD-C cable (9.5 ft)
    - Example: 2018-24 Volkswagen Tiguan
    
    <Note>
    VW/Audi vehicles require a gateway (J533) harness for openpilot longitudinal control. Camera harnesses are limited to stock ACC.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### OBD-C Cable

The OBD-C cable connects the comma four to your vehicle's OBD-II port and the vehicle harness.

#### Cable Options

**Standard OBD-C Cable (2 ft)**
- Used for most installations
- Shorter cable for compact routing
- Typical for Honda, Toyota, Hyundai, GM, FCA vehicles

**Long OBD-C Cable (9.5 ft)**
- Required for certain vehicles with distant harness locations
- Necessary for:
  - Most Volkswagen/Audi vehicles with J533 gateway harness
  - Some Ford vehicles (Q4 connector)
  - Vehicles with OBD-II port far from steering column

- Connects between battery and fuse box
- Professional installation recommended
- Battery protection prevents drain in storage
- Low voltage cutoff: ~11.8V

### Harness Box

The harness box is a protective enclosure for the vehicle harness connection.

- Houses the vehicle-specific harness connector
- Provides strain relief for cables
- Protects connections from moisture and debris
- Included with all harness purchases

### Mount

A windshield mount securely positions the comma four for optimal camera view.

#### Mounting Requirements

<Steps>
  <Step title="Clear View">
    The comma four must have an unobstructed view of the road and lane lines through the windshield.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver View">
    The driver-facing camera must have a clear view of the driver's face for Driver Monitoring.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stable Position">
    The mount must hold the device securely without vibration or movement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="No Obstruction">
    The device should not obstruct the driver's view of the road.
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Recommended Mounting Position

- **Location**: Behind the rearview mirror, centered on windshield
- **Height**: Top of device approximately at the top edge of the windshield
- **Angle**: Adjusted so road-facing camera has clear view of lane lines
- **Driver Camera**: Should have unobstructed view of driver's face

<Warning>
Check your local laws regarding windshield-mounted devices. Some jurisdictions have restrictions on placement or size.
</Warning>

## Installation Requirements

### DIY Installation

Many users successfully install sunnypilot hardware themselves:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Basic Tools Required">
    - Phillips screwdriver (for some trim panels)
    - Trim removal tools (optional, for cleaner installations)
    - Cable management clips/ties
  </Step>
  <Step title="Time Required">
    - First-time installation: 1-3 hours
    - Experienced users: 30-60 minutes
  </Step>
  <Step title="Difficulty Level">
    - **Easy**: Vehicles without comma power v3

Consider professional installation if:

- You're not comfortable working with vehicle electronics
- Your vehicle requires comma power v3 installation

## Troubleshooting Hardware Issues

### Common Hardware Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Device Not Powering On">
    **Possible Causes:**
    - OBD-C cable not fully connected
    - Vehicle OBD-II port not providing power
    - Faulty OBD-C cable
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Verify OBD-C cable connection at both ends
    - Test vehicle OBD-II port with another device
    - Try different OBD-C cable if available
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Poor Camera View">
    **Possible Causes:**
    - Incorrect mount positioning
    - Windshield obstruction (tint, damage)
    - Condensation on windshield or device
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Adjust mount position for clear view
    - Clean windshield and device cameras
    - Avoid mounting in tinted areas
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Harness Connection Errors">
    **Possible Causes:**
    - Wrong harness for vehicle variant
    - Loose harness connections
    - Damaged harness pins
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Verify correct harness type for your vehicle
    - Ensure all harness connections are fully seated
    - Inspect harness for physical damage
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="comma power v3 Not Working">
    **Possible Causes:**
    - Incorrect fuse tap installation
    - Battery voltage too low
    - Loose power connections
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Verify comma power v3 installation per instructions
    - Check vehicle battery voltage (should be >12V)
    - Inspect all power connections for tightness
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
