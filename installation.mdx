# Installation Guide

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about installing sunnypilot on your comma device.

## Hardware Requirements

### Supported Devices

sunnypilot supports the following comma devices:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="comma four" icon="microchip">
    Latest generation device with:
    - Improved thermal management
    - 77% reduced standby power (52 mW as of v0.10.4)
    - Enhanced performance
    - **Recommended for new installations**
  </Card>
  <Card title="comma 3/3X" icon="microchip">
    Previous generation with:
    - Full sunnypilot support
    - Proven reliability
    - Wide availability
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Older devices like comma two, EON, and grey/white panda are **not supported** by recent versions. Upgrade to comma four or comma 3/3X.
</Warning>

### Vehicle-Specific Hardware

Each vehicle requires specific hardware components:

- **Wiring Harness**: Vehicle-specific connector (Honda Nidec, Honda Bosch, Toyota, Ford Q3/Q4, GM, etc.)
- **OBD-C Cable**: Connects to your vehicle's OBD-II port (typically 2 ft, some vehicles need 9.5 ft)
- **comma power v3**: Provides clean power to the device (recommended for most vehicles)
- **Harness Box**: Houses the wiring connections safely
- **Mount**: Secures the comma device behind your rearview mirror

<Tip>
  When ordering from [comma.ai/shop](https://comma.ai/shop), select your exact vehicle make, model, and year to receive the correct harness and cables.
</Tip>

## Pre-Installation Checklist

Before installing sunnypilot, verify:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Vehicle Compatibility">
    Confirm your vehicle is on the [supported vehicles list](https://community.sunnypilot.ai/t/getting-started-using-sunnypilot-in-your-supported-car/251). Check that your specific year and trim level are supported.
    
    Your vehicle must have:
    - Factory adaptive cruise control (ACC)
    - Lane keeping assist or lane centering
    - Typically model year 2016 or newer (varies by manufacturer)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Hardware Acquisition">
    Ensure you have all required hardware:
    - comma device (four, 3, or 3X)
    - Correct vehicle-specific harness
    - OBD-C cable (correct length for your vehicle)
    - comma power v3 (if recommended for your vehicle)
    - Mounting hardware
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Internet Connection">
    You'll need WiFi access for:
    - Initial device setup
    - Downloading sunnypilot software
    - Software updates
    
    Consider using a mobile hotspot if your vehicle doesn't have WiFi.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Physical Installation

### Mounting the Device

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Mounting Location">
    Position the comma device behind your rearview mirror:
    - Camera should have a clear, unobstructed view of the road
    - Device should not interfere with driver's view
    - Keep driver-facing camera aimed at driver's seat
    - Ensure mount is secure and won't move while driving
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connect Wiring Harness">
    1. Locate your vehicle's forward-facing camera connector (typically behind rearview mirror)
    2. Disconnect the factory camera connector
    3. Connect your vehicle-specific harness inline:
       - Factory camera → Harness input
       - Harness output → comma device
    4. Secure connections to prevent disconnection from vibration
    
    <Warning>
      Different vehicles have different connector types (Nidec, Bosch, TSS2, etc.). Ensure you're using the correct harness for your specific vehicle.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connect OBD-II Cable">
    1. Locate your vehicle's OBD-II port (usually under dashboard near driver's knee)
    2. Firmly plug in the OBD-C cable
    3. Route the cable cleanly to the comma device
    4. Ensure cable doesn't interfere with pedals or steering
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connect comma power v3 (if applicable)">
    For vehicles that require comma power v3:
    1. Connect between OBD-II port and OBD-C cable
    2. Provides clean, regulated power to prevent device issues
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Cable Management">
    - Route all cables neatly along headliner and A-pillar
    - Use cable clips if needed to secure cables
    - Ensure no cables obstruct airbags or interfere with driving controls
    - Leave some slack for rearview mirror adjustment
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Software Installation

### Initial Device Setup

When you first power on your comma device:

<Steps>
  <Step title="First Boot">
    1. Turn on your vehicle's ignition (device powers on automatically)
    2. Wait for the device to boot (first boot takes 2-5 minutes)
    3. The setup wizard will appear on screen
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="WiFi Connection">
    1. Select your WiFi network from the list
    2. Enter the WiFi password
    3. Device will connect and verify internet access
    
    <Tip>
      If using a mobile hotspot, set it up before starting the installation process.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="comma Account">
    1. Create a new comma account or log in to existing account
    2. This enables access to comma connect for viewing your drives
    3. Your device will be registered to your account
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Accept Terms">
    1. Read and accept comma.ai terms of service
    2. Accept sunnypilot terms of service (after installing sunnypilot)
    3. Configure data collection preferences
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Installing sunnypilot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Software Settings">
    1. From the device home screen, tap the settings icon
    2. Navigate to **Software** settings
    3. Select **Uninstall openpilot**
    4. Confirm uninstallation and wait for it to complete
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Custom URL">
    After uninstallation, you'll see the setup screen:
    
    1. Look for the option to enter a custom software URL
    2. Enter the sunnypilot installer URL:
    
    ```bash
    https://smiskol.com/fork/sunnyhaibin
    ```
    
    3. Tap continue or confirm
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Branch">
    Choose your preferred sunnypilot branch (see [Branch Selection](#branch-selection) below):
    
    - **Stable branches** (e.g., `honda_c_stable`) - Recommended for daily use
    - **RC** (Release Candidate) - Latest tested features before stable release
    - **dev** - Development branch with cutting-edge features (may be unstable)
    
    <Note>
      The installer may show branch options specific to your vehicle or region. Select the appropriate stable branch for your vehicle type.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Download & Install">
    1. The device will download sunnypilot (this may take 5-15 minutes)
    2. Progress bar shows download status
    3. Device will automatically reboot after installation
    4. sunnypilot will start on next boot
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Branch Selection

Choosing the right branch is important for your sunnypilot experience:

### Recommended Branches

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Stable">
    **Stable Branches** (e.g., `honda_c_stable`, `toyota_stable`)
    
    - **Who it's for**: Most users, daily drivers
    - **Update frequency**: Monthly or quarterly
    - **Stability**: Highest - thoroughly tested
    - **Features**: Well-established, proven features
    - **Support**: Full community and official support
    
    Examples:
    - `honda_c_stable` - For Honda/Acura vehicles with comma four
    - `toyota_stable` - For Toyota/Lexus vehicles
    - `hyundai_stable` - For Hyundai/Kia/Genesis vehicles
    
    <Tip>
      Choose stable if you want a reliable, predictable experience.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Release Candidate (RC)">
    **RC Branch**
    
    - **Who it's for**: Enthusiasts who want new features early
    - **Update frequency**: Weekly or bi-weekly
    - **Stability**: High - tested but may have minor issues
    - **Features**: Latest tested features before stable release
    - **Support**: Community support, active testing
    
    <Note>
      RC branch gives you early access to new features while maintaining good stability. Report any issues to help improve the stable release.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Development (dev)">
    **dev Branch**
    
    - **Who it's for**: Testers, developers, advanced users
    - **Update frequency**: Daily or multiple times per week
    - **Stability**: Variable - may have bugs or breaking changes
    - **Features**: Cutting-edge, experimental features
    - **Support**: Limited - community only
    
    <Warning>
      Only use dev branch if you're comfortable with potential instability and want to help test new features. Not recommended for daily commuting.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Branch Naming Convention

Branches may include vehicle-specific identifiers:

- `honda_c_` - For comma four on Honda vehicles
- `toyota_` - For Toyota/Lexus vehicles
- `hyundai_` - For Hyundai/Kia/Genesis vehicles
- Regional variations may exist (e.g., `_eu` for European models)

For the most current branch recommendations, see the [community forum post on branch installations](https://community.sunnypilot.ai/t/recommended-branch-installations/235).

## Post-Installation Setup

### Device Calibration

After installing sunnypilot, the device must calibrate:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start Driving">
    - Begin driving normally with the device powered on
    - Calibration happens automatically in the background
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Optimal Calibration Conditions">
    For fastest calibration:
    - Drive on straight roads or highways
    - Ensure clear lane markings are visible
    - Maintain steady speed
    - Avoid frequent turns or lane changes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Calibration Progress">
    - Device displays calibration status on screen
    - Typically completes within 1-2 miles (2-3 km)
    - Green checkmark indicates calibration complete
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Do not attempt to engage sunnypilot until calibration is complete. The system will not engage until this step is finished.
</Warning>

### sunnypilot Configuration

After calibration, configure sunnypilot settings:

1. **Driver Monitoring**: Configure monitoring sensitivity (keep default for safety)
2. **Visual Preferences**: Customize UI elements (rainbow path, metrics display, etc.)
3. **Lateral Control**: Choose control method (keep default initially)
4. **Longitudinal Tuning**: Adjust following distance if needed
5. **sunnylink**: Optionally set up cloud configuration

<Tip>
  Start with default settings and make gradual adjustments as you become familiar with sunnypilot's behavior.
</Tip>

## Flashing comma four

If you need to reflash your comma four device:

### When to Reflash

- Device is stuck in a boot loop
- Severe software corruption
- Switching between major versions
- Factory reset needed

### Reflashing Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Flash Tool">
    Download the official comma flash tool from [comma.ai](https://comma.ai/)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Prepare Device">
    1. Power off the comma four
    2. Connect to your computer via USB-C
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Flash Mode">
    1. Hold the power button while connecting USB
    2. Device enters bootloader mode
    3. Computer should recognize the device
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Run Flash Tool">
    1. Execute the flash tool on your computer
    2. Select your device when prompted
    3. Choose the firmware version to flash
    4. Wait for flashing to complete (10-20 minutes)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reinstall sunnypilot">
    After flashing completes:
    1. Device will reboot with stock software
    2. Follow the [Installing sunnypilot](#installing-sunnypilot) steps above to reinstall
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only reflash as a last resort. Most issues can be resolved by reinstalling sunnypilot through the device settings.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

### Common Installation Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Device won't power on">
    **Possible causes:**
    - OBD-II connection not secure
    - Vehicle not in accessory or run mode
    - Faulty OBD-C cable
    - Dead vehicle battery
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Verify OBD-II connector is firmly seated
    - Turn vehicle to accessory or start engine
    - Try a different OBD-C cable
    - Check vehicle battery voltage (should be >12V)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can't download sunnypilot">
    **Possible causes:**
    - WiFi connection lost
    - Weak WiFi signal
    - Incorrect URL entered
    - Server temporarily unavailable
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Verify WiFi is connected and has internet access
    - Move closer to WiFi router or use mobile hotspot
    - Double-check the installer URL: `https://smiskol.com/fork/sunnyhaibin`
    - Wait and retry later if server is down
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Installation fails or errors out">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Insufficient storage space
    - Corrupted download
    - Software conflict
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Free up storage space on the device
    - Delete previous installation completely
    - Try a different branch
    - Factory reset device if necessary
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Calibration takes too long">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Not enough straight road driving
    - Poor lighting conditions
    - Dirty camera lens
    - Incorrect mounting angle
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Drive on highways with clear lane markings
    - Calibrate during daytime with good visibility
    - Clean the camera lens
    - Verify device mounting position and angle
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="sunnypilot won't engage">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Calibration incomplete
    - Vehicle speed too low
    - No lane lines detected
    - Driver monitoring alert active
    - Wrong harness for vehicle
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Complete calibration process
    - Accelerate to minimum speed (varies by vehicle)
    - Drive on roads with visible lane markings
    - Keep eyes on road to clear monitoring alerts
    - Verify correct harness for your specific vehicle
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Device keeps rebooting">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Unstable power supply
    - Overheating
    - Hardware failure
    - Software crash
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Install comma power v3 if not already using
    - Ensure proper airflow around device
    - Check for loose cable connections
    - Reinstall sunnypilot software
    - Check for device firmware updates
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Updating sunnypilot

sunnypilot can be updated through the device:

1. Connect to WiFi
2. Go to **Settings** → **Software**
3. Tap **Check for Updates**
4. If an update is available, tap **Update**
5. Device will download and install automatically
6. Device reboots after update completes

<Note>
  Updates typically preserve your settings and calibration. However, major version updates may require recalibration.
</Note>
