## Core Safety Requirements

sunnypilot is designed with two fundamental safety requirements:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Driver Must Always Be Capable of Immediate Manual Control" icon="hand">
    **You can ALWAYS retake control by:**
    
    - **Stepping on the brake pedal** - Immediately disengages the system
    - **Pressing the CANCEL button** - Disengages cruise control
    - **Turning the steering wheel** - Overrides lateral control
    - **Pressing accelerator** - Overrides longitudinal control (if "Disengage on Accelerator" enabled)
    
    **These override mechanisms work instantly and unconditionally.**
    
    <Warning>
      Never attempt to modify, disable, or interfere with these override mechanisms. They are critical safety features.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="2. Vehicle Must Not Alter Trajectory Too Quickly" icon="gauge">
    While engaged, actuators are constrained to operate within reasonable limits:
    
    - **Lateral limits**: ISO11270 and ISO15622 compliance
      - Maximum 0.9 seconds of actuation to achieve 1m lateral deviation
      - Prevents sudden steering movements
    
    - **Longitudinal limits**: Platform-specific acceleration/deceleration constraints
      - Gradual speed changes
      - Safe braking distances maintained
    
    These limits ensure the driver has time to safely react and intervene.
    
    **Additional Safety Details:**
    - [panda safety model](https://github.com/commaai/panda#safety-model)
    - [opendbc safety implementation](https://github.com/commaai/opendbc/tree/master/opendbc/safety/safety)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Driver Monitoring

sunnypilot includes **mandatory** driver monitoring to enforce driver alertness.

### How It Works

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Camera Monitoring">
    **Driver-Facing Camera:**
    - Continuously monitors your face and eye position
    - Detects when you're not looking at the road
    - Uses AI model to identify distracted or drowsy behavior
    
    **What It Detects:**
    - Eyes not on road
    - Phone usage
    - Looking down or away
    - Eyes closed (drowsiness)
    - Face not visible
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Alert Escalation">
    **Warning Levels:**
    
    1. **"Pay Attention"** (preDriverDistracted)
       - Gentle reminder to refocus on road
       - No audible alert
       - Brief visual message
    
    2. **"Pay Attention - Driver Distracted"** (promptDriverDistracted)
       - More urgent warning
       - Audible chime
       - Larger visual alert
    
    3. **"Driver Unresponsive"** (driverUnresponsive)
       - Critical alert
       - **System will disengage**
       - Loud warning chime
       - Requires immediate attention
    
    <Warning>
      Repeated driver monitoring violations may result in restricted access to sunnypilot features.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Best Practices">
    **To Avoid Alerts:**
    - Keep eyes on the road at all times
    - Don't look at the device screen for extended periods
    - Face forward in natural driving position
    - Ensure driver camera can see your face
    - Remove obstructions (sunglasses, hats that block view)
    - Don't use phone while system is engaged
    
    **What's Acceptable:**
    - Brief glances at mirrors
    - Quick checks of speedometer/gauges
    - Normal head movements while driving
    
    **What's Not Acceptable:**
    - Reading phone or texting
    - Extended looks at center console
    - Looking away from road for more than 2-3 seconds
    - Drowsy driving
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  **DO NOT attempt to disable, bypass, or defeat driver monitoring.** This violates safety requirements and is extremely dangerous.
</Warning>

## System Capabilities & Limitations

### What sunnypilot CAN Do

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Adaptive Cruise Control" icon="gauge-high">
    - Maintain set speed on highways
    - Follow lead vehicle at safe distance
    - Adjust speed for traffic
    - Stop and go in traffic (on supported vehicles)
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Automated Lane Centering" icon="road">
    - Keep vehicle centered in lane
    - Follow gentle curves
    - Track lane markings
    - Smooth steering inputs
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Safety Alerts" icon="bell">
    - Forward Collision Warning (FCW)
    - Lane Departure Warning (LDW)
    - Driver monitoring alerts
    - Speed limit warnings (if enabled)
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Enhanced Features" icon="star">
    - Custom driving models
    - Speed limit assistance
    - Lane change assistance (where supported)
    - Customizable behavior profiles
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### What sunnypilot CANNOT Do

<Warning>
  **sunnypilot is NOT:**
  - A self-driving system
  - Capable of navigating all road types
  - Able to handle all traffic situations
  - A replacement for an attentive driver
</Warning>

**Specific Limitations:**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Road Conditions" icon="road">
    **sunnypilot may struggle or fail with:**
    
    - Faded, missing, or unclear lane markings
    - Construction zones with altered lanes
    - Unpaved or dirt roads
    - Parking lots and private roads
    - Sharp curves or hairpin turns
    - Roads with no lane markings
    - Confusing intersections
    - Merging lanes or complex interchanges
    
    <Warning>
      Do not rely on sunnypilot in construction zones or areas with poor lane markings. Take manual control.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Weather Conditions" icon="cloud-rain">
    **Reduced or no functionality in:**
    
    - Heavy rain (camera visibility reduced)
    - Snow or ice (traction issues, covered lanes)
    - Fog (camera cannot see clearly)
    - Direct sun glare (camera blinded)
    - Wet or reflective roads (lane markings unclear)
    
    **What to Do:**
    - Take manual control in adverse weather
    - System may automatically disengage if conditions too poor
    - Do not engage in dangerous weather conditions
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Traffic Situations" icon="traffic-light">
    **System CANNOT handle:**
    
    - Traffic lights (system will not stop automatically)
    - Stop signs (system will not stop unless lead car present)
    - Pedestrians or cyclists (unless leading the vehicle)
    - Animals crossing road
    - Emergency vehicles
    - Complex intersections
    - Roundabouts (proceed with extreme caution)
    - Unprotected left turns
    - Merging into traffic without clear path
    
    <Warning>
      **CRITICAL:** sunnypilot does NOT detect or respond to traffic lights or stop signs. You MUST control the vehicle at intersections.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Vehicle Detection Limits" icon="car">
    **May not detect:**
    
    - Stationary vehicles at high speed
    - Motorcycles or bicycles
    - Vehicles cutting in suddenly
    - Vehicles far ahead but slowing
    - Cross traffic at intersections
    - Pedestrians or small objects
    
    **Detection Affected By:**
    - Lighting conditions (night, shadows, sun)
    - Vehicle color/contrast with background
    - Weather (rain, fog, snow)
    - Camera obstructions (dirt, ice, condensation)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## When NOT to Use sunnypilot

<Warning>
  **DO NOT engage sunnypilot in these situations:**
</Warning>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Road Conditions">
    ❌ **Don't Use On:**
    - Construction zones
    - Unmarked roads
    - Parking lots
    - Private roads or driveways
    - Gravel or unpaved roads
    - Roads with temporary lane configurations
    - Areas with confusing lane markings
    - Sharp curves or mountain roads (use extreme caution)
    - Roads under 25 mph speed limit (unless experienced)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Weather">
    ❌ **Don't Use During:**
    - Heavy rain or thunderstorms
    - Snow or ice (including light snow)
    - Dense fog
    - Severe sun glare
    - High winds affecting steering
    - Any conditions reducing visibility
    
    <Note>
      Even light weather can significantly reduce system performance. Use conservative judgment.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Vehicle State">
    ❌ **Don't Use When:**
    - Vehicle has mechanical issues
    - Tires are worn or underinflated
    - Alignment is off
    - Brakes are worn
    - Any warning lights on dashboard
    - Towing a trailer (unless tested and verified safe)
    - Carrying unusual loads affecting vehicle dynamics
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Driver State">
    ❌ **Don't Use If You Are:**
    - Drowsy or fatigued
    - Under influence of drugs, alcohol, or medications
    - Distracted or unable to focus
    - Emotionally upset or agitated
    - Unfamiliar with the system (practice in safe conditions first)
    - In a hurry (can lead to over-reliance on system)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Emergency Situations

### How to Take Control Immediately

<Steps>
  <Step title="Brake Firmly">
    Press brake pedal firmly. This instantly disengages all sunnypilot control.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Steer Manually">
    Turn steering wheel as needed. Your input overrides sunnypilot lateral control.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Accelerate if Needed">
    Press accelerator if you need to speed up (may disengage depending on settings).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Press Cancel">
    If time permits, press CANCEL button to fully disengage cruise control.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### After an Intervention

1. **Assess the situation** - Why did you need to intervene?
2. **Check system state** - Any alerts or errors on screen?
3. **Decide whether to re-engage** - Is it safe to do so?
4. **Report unexpected behavior** - Share on community forum to help improve sunnypilot

<Note>
  Frequent interventions are normal, especially when learning the system. They demonstrate you're monitoring properly.
</Note>

## Understanding Your Responsibility

sunnypilot is a **driver assistance** system, not a self-driving system.

<Note>
  **You are ALWAYS responsible for:**
  
  - Safe operation of your vehicle
  - Compliance with all traffic laws
  - Monitoring road conditions and hazards
  - Taking control when needed
  - Consequences of any accidents or violations
</Note>

**sunnypilot is a tool to assist you, not replace you.**

Think of it like:
- Power steering (assists steering, doesn't steer for you)
- Anti-lock brakes (assists braking, doesn't brake for you)
- Adaptive cruise control (maintains speed/distance, doesn't drive for you)

sunnypilot adds lane centering and enhanced cruise control, but **you remain the driver**.
